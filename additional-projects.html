Happily Ever After? Audio Syllabus with Sound Design & Narration

Challenge:

Transform a traditional written syllabus for a Fairy Tale Literature course into an engaging audio experience that not only conveys essential course information but builds genuine excitement for the subject matter, while ensuring accessibility for auditory learners and visually impaired students.

Approach:

Rather than simply reading the syllabus aloud, I designed a narrative audio journey that introduces the course's central question: How do Disney's sanitized fairy tales compare to the dark, morally complex originals by the Brothers Grimm and earlier folk storytellers?

The Audio Production:

Production Process:

Scriptwriting:

Adapted dry syllabus content into conversational, story-driven narration that mirrors the course's "light vs. dark" theme

Voiceover Recording:

Self-recorded narration with varied pacing and tone to maintain listener engagement across 8+ minutes

Sound Design:

Layered atmospheric sound effects matched to specific fairy tales:

  • Wolf howl for "Little Red Riding Hood"
  • Glass slipper clicks on marble for "Cinderella"
  • Forest ambience transitioning from ominous to magical

Music Integration:

Composed a score that shifts between haunting minor keys (Grimm's dark tales) and bright major keys (Disney's versions)

Audio Editing:

Mixed multiple tracks in Adobe Audition, balancing narration clarity with immersive soundscape

Impact:

The audio syllabus demonstrates that accessibility doesn't mean sacrificing creativity or engagement. By treating course information as a storytelling opportunity, the project models how instructional designers can make required content compelling rather than perfunctory.

Skills Demonstrated:

  • Audio script adaptation and narrative structure
  • Professional voiceover recording and delivery
  • Sound design and Foley integration
  • Music composition and layering
  • Adobe Audition audio editing (multitrack mixing, EQ, compression)
  • Accessibility-focused instructional design
  • Visual design for audio content presentation

Tools Used: Adobe Audition, Canva (visual design), royalty-free sound effects libraries, original music composition

Personal Note: This project ignited a love for voice work and sound design, proving that even "dry" content like syllabi can become engaging when approached creatively. It also deepened my appreciation for how audio can transform accessibility from checkbox compliance into genuine inclusion.

Bath Bomb Tutorial: Storyboard to Screen Video Production

Challenge:

Create a complete instructional video from concept through final production, demonstrating on-camera teaching presence, video production skills, and the ability to independently manage all aspects of multimedia content creation with limited equipment.

Project Scope:

A start-to-finish DIY bath bomb tutorial showcasing the complete video production workflow: storyboarding, filming, lighting design, audio recording, sound design, video editing, and final delivery.

Final Product:

Production Process:

Pre-Production:

  • Developed detailed storyboard mapping shot sequences, camera angles, and instructional flow
  • Planned lighting setup using available resources (ring lights, lamps, candles, natural light)
  • Scripted voiceover segments and on-camera teaching sections

Production:

  • Self-filmed all footage using strategic lighting placement to create a warm, inviting atmosphere
  • Recorded on-camera instruction demonstrating the bath bomb creation process step-by-step
  • Captured multiple angles and b-roll footage for visual variety

Post-Production:

  • Video editing in Adobe Premiere Pro (cutting, transitions, pacing)
  • Audio enhancement in Adobe Audition (voiceover recording, noise reduction, music integration)
  • Sound design: Layered background music and sound effects to enhance instructional tone
  • Color correction and visual polish

Why This Project Matters:

While video production isn't my primary passion, this project built critical skills and confidence for multimedia instructional design. It demonstrates the ability to independently manage complex production workflows, a valuable asset when creating video-based training modules or eLearning content that requires original footage.

The storyboard-to-screen process mirrors professional instructional video development, showing strategic planning, technical execution, and attention to both educational clarity and production quality.

Skills Demonstrated:

  • Instructional video storyboarding
  • On-camera teaching and presentation
  • Multi-source lighting design with limited equipment
  • Video filming and composition
  • Adobe Premiere Pro video editing
  • Adobe Audition audio production
  • Sound design and music integration
  • Self-directed project management (pre-production through delivery)

Tools Used: Adobe Premiere Pro, Adobe Audition, ring lights, camera, Canva (storyboard planning)

Personal Reflection: This project pushed me out of my comfort zone and proved that an effective instructional video doesn't require a professional studio, just strategic planning, creative problem-solving, and attention to learner needs. The confidence gained here directly influenced my ability to create video content for the Brain State Detective training program.
